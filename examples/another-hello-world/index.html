State of compute · 2026

The compute economy is leaving the building.

For two decades we centralized the world's intelligence into a few thousand warehouses. The grid, the water, and the math no longer agree. A quieter, distributed architecture is taking shape.

01The footprint
415TWh / 2024

Data centers now draw more electricity than entire industrialized nations.

Global data center consumption hit roughly 1.5% of all electricity on Earth — and demand grew 17% in 2025 alone, more than five times the rate of the wider grid.

SOURCE — IEA, World Energy Outlook 2026
02The trajectory
945TWh by 2030

Demand is forecast to more than double in five years.

The U.S. and China alone account for nearly 80% of the increase. AI workloads — currently a sliver of total load — will drive almost half of that growth.

SOURCE — IEA Energy & AI Report, 2026
03The hidden cost
1trillion liters of water · 2025

North American data centers drank a small sea last year.

A single hyperscale facility can evaporate 5 million gallons a day — the daily water of a 50,000-person city — purely to keep silicon below thermal throttle.

SOURCE — Reuters / Lincoln Institute, 2026
04The capital wave
$700Bhyperscaler capex · 2026

Four companies will spend more on compute this year than the GDP of Switzerland.

Amazon, Google, Meta and Microsoft are pouring nearly three-quarters of a trillion dollars into AI infrastructure — almost double 2025 — while free cash flow contracts.

SOURCE — Axios / CNBC capex tracking, Feb 2026
05The friction
20% of Ireland's grid

A handful of buildings are bending entire national grids.

Data centers consume more than a fifth of Ireland's electricity. Northern Virginia and parts of Texas face moratoria. The centralized model has hit physical limits — land, power, permits, water.

SOURCE — EirGrid; IEA regional impact analysis
06The pivot
650+live mesh networks

Compute is decentralizing into a planetary mesh.

Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks — DePIN — now run more than 650 production systems. GPU rendering, storage, wireless, inference — coordinated by protocols, not perimeters.

SOURCE — Messari DePIN tracker, Q1 2026
07The new market
14×edge market by 2034

Edge and distributed compute are scaling faster than any cloud category in history.

From $21B in 2025 toward an estimated $264B–$1.5T by mid-decade. By 2027 nearly 60% of organizations will run hybrid edge architectures by default.

SOURCE — Fortune Business Insights · GMI · Roots Analysis
08The shift
A quieter grid.

From a few hundred warehouses — to billions of nodes.

The next decade of computing won't be built in deserts and cooled with aquifers. It will live inside laptops, base stations, GPUs at the edge, and homes wired into a coordinated mesh. Cheaper, cooler, more resilient — and closer to the people it serves.

SOURCE — Synthesis · IEA · Messari · Reuters · Brookings

The macro picture, in one line.

We're spending three-quarters of a trillion dollars per year to centralize what physics, water, and the grid are quietly forcing back to the edge.
